## 1. Executive Summary & Objective

Milestone P successfully implement the **Wilderness Exploration & Hexcrawl Engine**, transposing the deterministic time-tracking and resource management of the Dungeon Clock (Milestone O) into the Overworld. The system manages a theoretically infinite hex grid where time is measured in 4-hour **Watches**. The AI Narrator is mechanically constrained; it voices outcomes generated by the engine but cannot invent terrain features, weather states, or travel progress.

---

## 2. Architecture Overview

The engine follows a strict three-tier separation of concerns:
- **State Tier (Prisma):** Canonical truth for party position, hex discovery, weather, and time.
- **Rules Tier (Pure Functions):** Zero-I/O logic in `lib/rules/wilderness.ts` for movement calculation, foraging resolution, and weather transitions.
- **Narrative Tier (AI Tool):** The `executeTravelWatch` tool in `lib/ai/tools/wilderness.ts` acts as the sole authorized mutator, binding the rules to the database.

---

## 3. Data Schema & State Management

### 3.1 `WildernessMap` Model
Tracks the persistent state of the world grid. Hexes use a cube coordinate system `(q, r)` and are generated on-demand with seeded determinism.
- **Seeded Identity:** `SHA1(campaignId + ":" + q + ":" + r)` ensures geography never drifts.
- **Visibility:** `discovered` (occupied) vs. `scouted` (adjacent/revealed).

### 3.2 `TravelState` Model
Single-record temporal/spatial context for the campaign.
- **Temporal Atomic Unit:** `currentWatch` (0–5).
- **Spatial Atomic Unit:** `currentQ`, `currentR`.
- **Mechanics:** Tracks `watchesTraveledToday` for exhaustion gating and `partialHexProgress` for "Slow" pace movement.

---

## 4. Functional Specification: `executeTravelWatch`

The `executeTravelWatch` tool is the primary engine interface. It executes the following atomic logic:

1.  **Temporal Validation:** Refuses any non-`rest` action during the mandatory Night Watch (index 5).
2.  **Weather Recalculation:** Every 6 watches, transitions weather states along a severity ladder (Clear → Overcast → Rain → Storm → Fog → Snow).
3.  **Action Resolution:**
    - `travel`: Updates hex position based on Pace (Slow/Normal/Fast) and Weather penalties (Movement blocked in severe storms/coast).
    - `forage`: Survival check vs. Terrain-based DC; success modifies `PartyInventory.rations`.
    - `scout`: Reveals adjacent hexes without advancing the party.
4.  **Encounter Engine:** Triggers 1d6 encounter checks on destination entry (Threshold: 1 for normal, 2 for dangerous terrain).
5.  **Resource Decay:** Deducts 1 ration from `PartyInventory` every 6 watches.
